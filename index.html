About Me

I enjoy helping people; it's just in my nature...


I want others to succeed, and I like when they succeed because of something I've done to help.


Naturally this passion for helping people lead me to teaching.


I was an elementary school teacher for 10 years. I loved helping students master a new concept. Kids learning to love math, becoming readers, discovering science... all of that was great!


However I found myself looking for more. I wanted to know what else existed outside of teaching... it was all I knew. It was all I had ever seen myself doing.


Then one day my husband suggested coding.


ME?


You're a natural. You break down tasks all the time. You can teach a 10 year to divide! Now just teach the computers.


It wasn't something that had entered my world until that moment.


Me?


Why not me?


So I played around with Codeacademy for a few weeks, and just searched around online to see what was out there.


I found that there was a lot out there.


I found the U of A Coding Bootcamp. I called them. They told me I'd have to pass a test and a screening interview to be accepted into the program.


Me?


I thought I was doomed. I knew NOTHING about coding. How would I pass?


But then I went for it. I decided that was exactly what I needed to do. If I could pass the test and the interview then I knew it was the right choice.


If not?


I can always try something else. But this was a place to start. A place to try something new. To be creative. To challenge myself.


I went for it and I passed. They said I did great. (Maybe they tell everyone that?!?)


I was shocked, but also not. Of course I passed.


Why NOT me?


I'm smart. I can learn anything I want. Nothing holding me back....


I began the boot camp in January. I learned so much. So much, so fast.


So much coding... So many languages, and libraries, and programs, and data bases... and the syntax for all of these...


And so much more.


I am a smart person (I have to remind myself of this often). This boot camp was the biggest challenge I've met.


Every single thing was hard for me. I'm not used to that. I had ZERO prior experience.


The weird thing was I never wanted to give up. Never. I just wanted to figure things out. I wanted to know why things weren't working.


Or why they were.


I wanted to figure it out.


Don't get me wrong. I have banged my head against the wall so many times. Like every day. Sometimes by the minute...


But I never wanted to quit. I just wanted to figure things out. I wanted to know why things worked. Or didn't.


I think this makes me a coder.


I could have hated this. I had no experience.... I really had no idea what I was getting myself into. There were many, many times I could have thought "Why did I do this? What was I thinking..."


I could have just tried to "survive" the 6 months and then give up.


However I did survive. And I don't want to give up.


I have so much more to learn. I can't stop now. I know too much. My brain has the beginnings of so much knowledge. I can't stop now.


I want to dig in. Learn more. Fill in the gaps that I may have missed in boot camp.... I want experience. I want to practice.


I want to know more.
